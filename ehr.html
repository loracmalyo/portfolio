EHR App UI Design

Company: University Assignment, Healthcare

Position: UX/UI and graphic designer

Date: Nov 2018 - Dec 2018

Services: UX/UI Design, Graphic Design

‍

Assignment Description

This project is created for IAT481, a special topic design course that focuses on the healthcare field. As a team of 4, we have to find and solve a design problem within 6 weeks, and during the process, we have to present our milestone to the class for critique and feedback.

‍

Problem

The Electronic Health Record (EHR) is a common application used by doctors and nurses to organize patient information inside a clinic. After plenty of research, our team identified that the complex and outdated UI design in the current EHR is frustrating for health providers to use.

Information overflow and complex interactions are some major UX/UI issues that we recognized. We also noticed that this EHR application should be moved to tablets to improve the convenience for the clinic staff and improve face-to-face interaction between staff and patients.

‍

Process

The complex EHR interface was broken down into several pages in order to simplify the layout and reduce the amount of information on a single page. A refreshing colour palette of blue and green was chosen to reveal a clean and professional look.

Interactions are made explicit with very clear prompts to guide users on where to tap or type. The overall font hierarchy, buttons designs and icon placements have made the new design more approachable and easy to learn.

We also implemented feedback and suggestions from doctors and other clinic staff throughout the design process, carefully restructured the information architecture and redesigned the interfaces.

‍

Outcome

Our redesigned EHR interface received highly rated comments from our professor and the guest critic from the healthcare field. The design of the final deliverables is greatly appreciated, including a Figma working prototype, report booklet and presentation slides.
